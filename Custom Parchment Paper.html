Frequently Asked Questions

Is parchment paper just baking paper?

While both parchment paper and baking paper are used for cooking and baking, they have distinct differences:

Parchment Paper: Made from wood pulp and chemically treated, it's highly heat-resistant, non-stick, and reusable.
Baking Paper: A less expensive, single-use paper often coated with silicone.

So, while both can be used for similar purposes, parchment paper offers superior performance and durability.

Can parchment paper be composted?

Yes, our parchment paper is eco-friendly and home compostable, breaking down within 3 months in home composting conditions.

Unique Designs: Add your logo, brand message, artwork, and QR codes.
Custom Sizes and Shapes: Tailor the paper to your specific needs.

Is brown or white parchment paper better?

Both brown and white parchment paper are suitable for most cooking and baking tasks. The primary difference lies in their production process:

Brown Parchment Paper: Unbleached, more eco-friendly, and often preferred by those who prioritize sustainability.
White Parchment Paper: Bleached, often perceived as more visually appealing, and can be slightly more heat-resistant.

Ultimately, the best choice depends on your personal preferences and needs. Both offer excellent performance in terms of heat resistance, non-stick properties, and food safety.

How many times can you reuse parchment paper?

You can typically reuse parchment paper 2-3 times. However, the number of times depends on how heavily it's been used and the type of food it was in contact with.

Greasiness: If heavily greased, discard after one use.
Tears and Punctures: Damaged paper should not be reused.
Discoloration: Replace if it becomes discolored or brittle.
